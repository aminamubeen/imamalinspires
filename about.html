The Project

Why Imam Ali Inspires?

This site was born from a simple need — a place to sit quietly with the words of Imam Ali (عليه السلام) and let them speak to whatever you're carrying that day. No distractions, no noise. Just the wisdom.

The sayings here are drawn from the Nahj al-Balagha (Peak of Eloquence), a collection of sermons, letters, and short sayings attributed to Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (AS), compiled in the 10th century CE by Sharif al-Radi. It remains one of the most celebrated works of Arabic prose and Islamic thought.

The mood-based discovery came from realising that sometimes you don't know which quote you need — you only know how you feel. So the site meets you there.

Who Was Imam Ali (AS)?

The Lion of God

Ali ibn Abi Talib (600–661 CE) was the cousin and son-in-law of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), the first Imam of the Shia tradition, and the fourth Caliph in Sunni history.

He is revered across the Islamic world for his profound knowledge, his sense of justice, his asceticism, and his eloquence — a combination so rare that his words have echoed across fourteen centuries.

The Source

Nahj al-Balagha

Nahj al-Balagha gathers 241 sermons, 79 letters, and 480 short sayings. The collection was compiled around 1000 CE by Sharif al-Radi, a scholar who sought to preserve the linguistic and spiritual legacy of the Imam's words.

The sayings on this site draw from those 480 aphorisms — each one a complete thought, dense with meaning, timeless in relevance.

The Developer
